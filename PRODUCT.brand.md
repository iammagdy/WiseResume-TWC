# Product (Brand / Landing)

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective users discovering WiseResume for the first time — curious job seekers, career changers, or professionals who've heard of it through referral or search. They arrive skeptical: "Is this actually different?" They need to be convinced in seconds, not minutes. Secondary audience: hiring managers evaluating WiseResume for teams (WiseHire angle).

## Product Purpose

The landing and marketing surfaces exist to convert curiosity into conviction. The job is to communicate WiseResume's value — AI-powered career tools that give users a real edge — in a way that feels credible, modern, and earned. Not a pitch deck. A demonstration of craft that makes the visitor feel the product before they even sign up.

## Brand Personality

Confident, Smart, Empowering. The brand voice on the landing should feel like a career expert who's seen the other tools and knows they're better — without saying it directly. Show, don't tell.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing pages:** Hero → feature grid → pricing → CTA. Beige/cream backgrounds, Inter everywhere, purple-to-blue gradients, cards nested in cards. Indistinguishable from any other B2B tool.
- **Hustle-culture job content:** Aggressive "Land your dream job NOW" energy. Feels cheap and desperate.
- **Over-corporate career sites (LinkedIn, Indeed marketing):** Dense, blue, institutional. Zero delight.
- **AI product stereotype:** Gradient text, glassmorphism hero, "the future of X" headline. Signals low effort.

## Design Principles

1. **The landing IS the product demo** — Visual quality on the marketing page is the first signal of what the product delivers. Ship it to the same standard as the app.
2. **Credibility through specificity** — Real screenshots, real numbers, real user outcomes. No generic "10x your career" claims without evidence.
3. **Crimson as identity, not decoration** — The WiseResume brand red (#9E1B22) should read as a deliberate, ownable identity choice — not just a primary button color.
4. **Motion earns attention** — Animations exist to direct focus and communicate capability, not to fill time. Every motion beat should have a purpose.
5. **Convert through confidence** — A visitor who feels impressed by the landing will trust the product. Design for admiration first, conversion follows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Reduced-motion alternatives for all scroll-driven and entrance animations (Lenis + Framer Motion already in use). Ensure contrast holds at the crimson brand color on both light and dark landing variants.
