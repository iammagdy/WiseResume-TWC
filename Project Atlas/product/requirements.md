# Product

## Register

product

## Users

All-stage career travelers: active job seekers building and tailoring resumes for specific roles, career changers and students navigating unfamiliar territory, and employed professionals maintaining their brand proactively. Users arrive stressed, time-pressured, or uncertain — they need confidence, not complexity. Primary context is focused task sessions: editing a resume, tailoring to a job description, preparing for an interview, tracking applications.

## Product Purpose

WiseResume is an AI-powered career toolkit — resume builder, cover letter editor, interview coach, portfolio builder, job tracker, and application manager in one. It exists to give every user the edge of having a senior career coach on demand. Success looks like: a user landing an interview they wouldn't have gotten without it.

## Brand Personality

Confident, Smart, Empowering. The voice is direct and encouraging — never condescending, never overly casual. It speaks like a trusted advisor who respects the user's intelligence and their time.

## Anti-references

- **Generic job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn):** Bland, corporate, blue-heavy, information-dense. Feels like a utility, not a tool you enjoy using.
- **Over-designed minimalist SaaS (Notion/Linear aesthetic):** Cold, overly monochrome, developer-coded. Too aloof for a product that deals with people's livelihoods.
- **Cheap resume template sites:** Cluttered, low-trust, aggressive upsells, visually dated. The opposite of the confidence WiseResume should project.

## Design Principles

1. **Expert confidence, not corporate comfort** — Every surface should feel like it was designed by someone who knows what good looks like. Avoid safe, committee-approved defaults.
2. **Progress, not perfection** — UI should communicate forward momentum. Users should always feel one step closer to their goal, never stuck or lost.
3. **Earn trust through craft** — The quality of the interface signals the quality of the output. A sloppy UI undermines trust in AI-generated documents.
4. **Clarity over feature density** — The product has many features; the UI should reveal them progressively. Primary tasks must never compete with secondary ones.
5. **Human at the center** — This is about people's careers. Tone, copy, and visual hierarchy should acknowledge the emotional weight of job searching — reassuring, never cold.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Support reduced motion (Framer Motion `useReducedMotion` already integrated). Color contrast must hold in both light and dark themes. Two-product system (WiseResume crimson / WiseHire blue) must not rely on color alone to differentiate.
